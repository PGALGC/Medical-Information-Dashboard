Welcome to Your Medical Dashboard

This app is a personal health tracker Everything is stored privately in your browser — no data is sent to any server or the internet.

What the App Contains
Surgery / Clinic DetailsStore your doctor and clinic contact info & address.
Patient DetailsYour personal info, NHS & hospital numbers, next of kin.
Medication ListTrack all your medicines, dosage and timing.
BP & ECG ReadingsLog blood pressure, pulse, ECG & diagnosis notes.
General NotesRecord operations, appointments or health events by date.
Data ManagementBackup, restore and export your data to Excel or text.
Auto-Save

All changes save automatically to your browser's local storage as you type. You will never lose data from typing — but use Backup All Data regularly to keep a safe copy on your device.

Quick Actions Button (top-right of header)

Click the Quick Actions button in the top-right corner of the green header bar to open a dropdown menu with four options:

How To Use This App

Opens this guide. Navigate using the tabs at the top to jump to any section.

Complete Capture Details

Generates a full formatted text summary of all your data — profile, doctor, medicines, BP readings, and notes. Inside the summary window you can:

  • Copy to Clipboard — pastes the full summary to your clipboard so you can paste it into an email or message.
  • Print Summary — opens your browser's print dialog to print the summary on paper.
  • Save as Text File — downloads the summary as a .txt file to your device.
Theme Selection

Change the colour scheme of the entire app. Pick from a range of colour themes (Green, Blue, Purple, Red, etc.). The theme is saved and restored every time you open the app.

Data Management

See the Data & Backup tab in this guide for full details.

Surgery / Clinic Details Section

Store your GP surgery or clinic's contact details here. This info appears in the Complete Capture report.

1
Fill in the form fields

Enter the Doctor's Name, Surgery/Clinic Name, Phone Number, and full address (House, Line 1, Line 2, Town, County, Postcode).

2
Click "Save Surgery Details"

The green button at the bottom saves all fields. The form collapses into a neat display view.

Options dropdown (⚙ button)

Click the green Options button in the section header to reveal:

  • Edit Details — re-opens the form so you can make changes.
  • Delete Details — clears all surgery/clinic fields permanently (after confirmation).
Patient Details Section

Store your own personal and medical identification details here.

1
Fill in your details
  • Full Name — pre-filled as "PATIENT".
  • Date of Birth — use the date picker.
  • Phone Number — your personal phone.
  • NHS Number — your 10-digit NHS number.
  • Hospital Number — your hospital patient ID.
  • Next of Kin Name — emergency contact name.
  • Next of Kin Phone — emergency contact number.
  • Address — your home address (House, Lines, Town, County, Postcode).
2
Click "Save Patient Details"

Saves all fields and shows a summary display.

Options dropdown
  • Edit Details — reopen the form to update info.
  • Delete Details — wipe all patient fields (after confirmation).
Medication List Section

Track all your current medications in a table. Each row is one medicine.

1
Click "Add Medicine"

The green Add Medicine button (bottom-right of the section) adds a new blank row to the table.

2
Fill in each column
  • Medicine Name — type the name of the drug (e.g. Amlodipine).
  • Dosage (mg) — enter the milligram amount (e.g. 5mg).
  • Due Time / Frequency — enter when it's taken (e.g. "Morning", "8am & 8pm", "Once daily").

Changes save automatically as you type — no save button needed.

3
Delete a row

Click the red trash icon on any row to delete that medicine (you will be asked to confirm).

Options dropdown
  • Export Medicines to Excel — downloads just the medicines table as an .xlsx spreadsheet.
  • Clear All Medicines — removes every medicine in one go (after confirmation).
Blood Pressure & ECG Readings Section

Log every blood pressure measurement and ECG result. Each row is one reading.

1
Click "Add BP Reading"

The green button at the bottom-right adds a new row, pre-filled with today's date and current time.

2
Fill in each column
  • DATE — date of the reading (date picker, defaults to today).
  • TIME — time of the reading (defaults to now).
  • SYSTOLIC — upper BP number (e.g. 120).
  • DIASTOLIC — lower BP number (e.g. 80).
  • PULSE — heart rate in bpm.
  • SITTING DOWN — BP reading taken while seated (Yes / No / value).
  • STANDING UP — BP reading taken while standing (Yes / No / value).
  • ECG MONITOR — result or reference from ECG monitor if used.
  • TYPE DIAGNOSIS — any diagnosis note (e.g. "Normal", "Stage 1 Hypertension").

All fields save automatically as you type.

3
Delete a row

Click the red trash icon to remove a reading (confirmation required).

Options dropdown
  • Export BP Readings to Excel — downloads just the BP table as .xlsx.
  • Clear All BP Readings — removes every reading (confirmation required).
The table scrolls horizontally on smaller screens — swipe left/right to see all columns.
General Notes Section

Found inside the BP & ECG section. Use this to log any medical events — operations, hospital visits, appointments, or observations.

1
Click "Add Row"

The green Add Row button creates a new entry pre-filled with today's date.

2
Fill in each column
  • Date — click the date picker to choose the date of the event. Click the Date column header to toggle sort order (newest first ▼ or oldest first ▲).
  • Type of Operation — describe the type of event (e.g. "Hip Surgery", "GP Appointment", "Blood Test").
  • Notes — any additional details, outcomes or follow-ups.

Data saves automatically as you type.

3
Delete a row

Click the red trash icon to remove that note (confirmation required).

Data Management (via Quick Actions → Data Management)

All your data lives in your browser. Use these tools to keep it safe and portable.

Backup All Data

Downloads a .json file containing everything — medicines, BP readings, general notes, patient details, surgery details, and theme. Store this file safely on your computer or cloud storage. Do this regularly!

Restore from Backup

Click this and select a previously saved .json backup file. All data will be restored from that file, replacing whatever is currently in the app.

Export All to Excel (Single Sheet)

Downloads all your data — profile, medicines, BP readings, and general notes — into a single .xlsx spreadsheet. Useful for sharing with a doctor or keeping a printable record.

Clear All Data

⚠ Warning: This permanently deletes ALL data including medicines, BP readings, notes, and profile info. This cannot be undone. Always backup first!

Your data never leaves your device. It is stored entirely in your browser's local storage and is not sent anywhere online.
Tips & Best Practices
Back up regularly

Use Quick Actions → Data Management → Backup All Data at least once a week. Save the file to your Downloads folder, Google Drive, or email it to yourself.

Works on mobile

The app is fully responsive. On phones, sections stack vertically and wide tables scroll horizontally with a swipe.

Printing for a doctor

Use Quick Actions → Complete Capture Details → Print Summary to print a clean A4 summary of all your health information to bring to an appointment.

Personalise the look

Use Quick Actions → Theme Selection to pick a colour scheme that is easy on your eyes.

Don't clear browser data

Clearing your browser's cache or cookies will erase all app data. Always backup before clearing browser storage.

Excel exports

Individual sections (Medicines, BP Readings) can be exported using their own Options → Export to Excel button. The Data Management panel exports everything into one sheet.
